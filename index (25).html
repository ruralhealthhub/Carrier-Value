A Partnership Proposal for Health Plans

Bring specialty care to your members — before it becomes an emergency.

Rural Health Hub gives your members local access to specialty expertise — for early detection, timely intervention, and treatment adherence — while steering advanced care in-network and managing utilization across the full episode.

The Opportunity

Your rural members face the widest gaps in specialty access — and that gap is expensive.

When specialists are hours away and wait times stretch for weeks, care gets delayed. Conditions that could have been caught and managed early instead surface in the emergency room or as an avoidable admission — the most costly settings in the system. That is a member-experience problem and a total-cost-of-care problem at the same time.

Distance & workforce gaps

Specialist shortages and long travel mean members defer care — or skip it entirely — until symptoms escalate.

Delayed detection

Without timely specialty input, the window for early, lower-cost intervention closes and conditions progress.

Avoidable acute spend

Deferred care converts into ER visits, admissions, and readmissions — cost that lands squarely on the plan.

The Platform

One connected platform for the entire arc of a member’s care.

Rural Health Hub combines on-demand specialty access with longitudinal management and in-network navigation. It deploys on top of your existing network as a turnkey solution. Click each stage to see how it works.

Local clinicians and members get rapid access to specialty expertise — without the weeks-long wait or the long drive. Questions get answered while the member is still in front of a care team, so issues are addressed early instead of escalating.

The result is faster diagnosis, earlier intervention, and fewer situations that deteriorate into emergencies simply because help was too far away.

Beyond the one-time consult, the platform supports ongoing management of chronic and complex conditions in the member’s own community. Consistent follow-up keeps treatment on track and surfaces problems before they require acute care.

  • Continuity that reduces fragmentation across providers and settings
  • Support for treatment adherence and self-management
  • Early identification of members trending toward higher acuity

When a member genuinely needs advanced or higher-acuity care, the platform guides them to your in-network facilities and specialists. That protects continuity for the member and keeps care within the plan’s contracted network — reducing leakage and out-of-network spend.

Members reach the right level of care deliberately, rather than defaulting to the nearest ER.

The platform manages members through the full episode — preparing them appropriately before a procedure or admission and supporting recovery afterward. Well-prepared, well-followed patients have fewer complications, fewer readmissions, and a smoother return to local care.

This peri-procedural wrap-around is where avoidable expense is most often prevented.

Expanded access only helps a plan if it is paired with disciplined utilization. Rural Health Hub does both: it opens the front door to early care while steering members away from avoidable acute spend and out-of-network leakage.

Fewer avoidable ER visits

Early, local access gives members an alternative to the emergency room for issues that don’t belong there.

Prevented admissions & readmissions

Proactive management and peri-procedural support keep manageable conditions from becoming inpatient events.

Reduced network leakage

In-network steering keeps advanced care — and its spend — within your contracted network.

Lower total cost of care

Shifting spend from acute settings to early, local management bends the cost curve over the full episode.

Rural defaults — rural members have less local access, driving materially higher avoidable acute use. Replace each with your plan’s own actuals:

  • Avoidable ER visits — ~130 per 1,000 / yr. Rural ED visit rate ≈645 per 1,000 (JAMA Network Open, 2019) × ~20% avoidable; rural preventable ED visits run ~44–45% above urban (Johnston et al., JGIM 2021).
  • Avoidable admissions — ~15 per 1,000 / yr. Rural ambulatory care–sensitive (preventable) hospitalizations exceed urban (AHRQ; Johnston et al., 2021); national average ≈12 per 1,000.
  • Avg cost per avoidable ER visit — ~$1,700. National median allowed amount (UnitedHealthcare, 2023); held at national average (rural/urban per-unit cost differences are smaller and variable).
  • Avg cost per avoidable admission — ~$16,500. National mean inpatient stay (AHRQ–HCUP, 2022; ≈$16,667); held at national average.
  • Share addressable by early access — 20%. A modeling assumption, not a benchmark — set to your plan’s experience.

Urban defaults — urban utilization tracks close to the U.S. national average. Replace each with your plan’s own actuals:

  • Avoidable ER visits — ~85 per 1,000 / yr. Urban ED visit rate ≈428 per 1,000 (JAMA Network Open, 2019) × ~20% avoidable (published avoidable range ~13–27%).
  • Avoidable admissions — ~11 per 1,000 / yr. Near the national AHRQ avoidable-hospitalization average (≈12 per 1,000), slightly below rural.
  • Avg cost per avoidable ER visit — ~$1,700. UnitedHealthcare 2023 median allowed amount per ER visit.
  • Avg cost per avoidable admission — ~$16,500. National mean inpatient stay (AHRQ–HCUP, 2022; ≈$16,667).
  • Share addressable by early access — 20%. A modeling assumption, not a benchmark — set to your plan’s experience.

Rates vary by age mix, payer, and region. Per-unit costs are held at national averages because rural/urban cost differences are smaller and more variable than utilization differences. Figures are illustrative and exclude readmission and out-of-network leakage savings.
